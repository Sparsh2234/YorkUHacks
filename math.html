Pythagorean Theorem

This module will teach you how to use Pythagorean Theorem to solve interesting math problems!

The purpose of using Pythagorean Theorem is to calcute the length of an unkown side in a right angled triangle

Steps

Before attempting the question using Pythagorean Theorem, check to see if the triangle has a 90 degree angle.

If a 90 degree angle is not present, Pythagorean Theorem can't be applied to this question.

Idetify the side opposite to the right angle, this will be your hypotenuse. The other two sides will be the legs of the triangle.

In this step we will assign variables to the sides of the triangle. The hypotenuse will be labelled as c and the other two sides will be a and b. The variables for the legs of the traingle are interchangeable.

The formula for Pythagorean Theorem is a^2 + b^2 = c^2. By substituting in the values of any two variables, we can find the value of the unknown term.
